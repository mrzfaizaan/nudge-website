Nudge vs Notion

A lightweight HUD for your tasks. Or: an all-in-one workspace with wikis, databases, AI, and collaboration. These are different tools built for different problems.

The short answer:

Notion is a better wiki. A better database. A better team hub. Nudge is a better to-do list for focused solo work. Notion replaces your docs, spreadsheets, and project boards. Nudge replaces your sticky notes and alt-tabbing. They aren't competitors — they solve different problems. But if you're using Notion just for task management, you might be paying $120/year for a tool that weighs 337 MB and loads slower than a text file.

Privacy & Data Ownership

Nudge reads a single .md file on your machine. No servers. No accounts. No export. You control the file. Version control it. Back it up. It's yours.

Notion stores everything on their cloud. Your wikis, databases, meeting notes, and tasks live on Notion's servers. If Notion changes pricing, removes features, or has an outage, your workspace is affected. Export is available but not seamless.

Different models. Notion's cloud is a feature for teams. Nudge's local file is a feature for individuals.

Who should choose which

Choose Nudge if

  • ✓ You just need a task list, not a workspace
  • ✓ You want tasks visible while working in other apps
  • ✓ You hate subscriptions and cloud lock-in
  • ✓ Speed matters — you want sub-100ms task switching
  • ✓ You already write notes in .md files
  • ✓ You want a tool that stays out of your way

Choose Notion if

  • ✓ You need wikis, databases, and rich documents
  • ✓ Your team collaborates in a shared workspace
  • ✓ You want AI-generated docs and meeting notes
  • ✓ You need project management and kanban boards
  • ✓ You manage knowledge across a company
  • ✓ The free plan covers your needs

Nudge won't replace Notion. It replaces the to-do list you're alt-tabbing to inside Notion.
